# Internal Lending Policy — Sample Bank

Version: 2024-Q1 | Approved by: Risk Committee | Next Review: 2025-Q1

## Single Borrower Exposure Limit

- Maximum exposure to a single borrower: 15% of Capital Funds (Tier 1 + Tier 2)
- Maximum exposure to a borrower group: 25% of Capital Funds
- For infrastructure projects: up to 25% (single) and 40% (group) with board approval

## NPA Early Warning Signals (EWS)

Banks must activate EWS monitoring when:
- Account shows DPD >= 15 days for 2 consecutive months
- Credit score drops by more than 50 points in 3 months
- Utilisation of working capital limit exceeds 90% continuously
- Return of cheques/ECS more than twice in a quarter
- Multiple loan enquiries from the borrower on credit bureaus

Upon EWS trigger, Relationship Manager must contact borrower within 5 business days.

## Interest Rate Policy

Base Rate: EBLR (External Benchmark Lending Rate) = RBI Repo Rate + 2.65%
Current EBLR: 9.15% (as of Q1 2024)

Spreads above EBLR:
- AAA-rated corporates: 0–0.50%
- AA-rated: 0.50–1.00%
- A-rated: 1.00–2.00%
- BBB and below: 2.00–4.00%
- Retail (Home): 0.25–1.00%
- Retail (Personal): 2.00–8.00%
