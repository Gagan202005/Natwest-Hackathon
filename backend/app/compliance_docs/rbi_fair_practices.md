# RBI Fair Practices Code for Lenders

Source: RBI Master Circular DBOD.No.Leg.BC.55/09.07.005/2012-13

## Loan Applications and Processing

- Banks must acknowledge all loan applications and provide a time frame for disposal
- Loan application forms must clearly indicate documents required
- Credit appraisal must be based on credit information and not on extraneous considerations
- Rejection of loan applications must be communicated with reason (where feasible)

## Loan Appraisal and Terms/Conditions

- Sanction letter must be issued to the borrower
- Terms and conditions (interest rate, fees, pre-payment charges) must be in the loan agreement
- Changes to terms must be communicated to the borrower with sufficient notice
- Borrowers must be informed of annualised interest rates

## Interest Rate Policy

- Banks must adopt an interest rate model based on relevant factors
- Rate of interest must be communicated clearly in the loan agreement
- For floating rate loans, the benchmark (MCLR, EBLR) and spread must be disclosed
- Excessive interest rates not commensurate with risk profile of the borrower are prohibited

## Disbursement

- Disbursement must be as per terms and conditions of sanction
- Delays without cause are prohibited

## Post-Disbursement Supervision

- Banks should not interfere with borrower's day-to-day management unless covenants are breached
- Regular inspection of security only in warranted cases

## Recovery

- Recovery agents must be properly trained and authorised
- No harassment, intimidation, or use of muscle power in recovery
- Recovery calls only between 7 AM and 7 PM
- All recovery communications must be documented

## Grievance Redressal

- Banks must have a designated grievance redressal officer
- Complaints must be resolved within 30 days
- If not resolved, borrower can approach Banking Ombudsman

## Common Violations in Audit

- Charging pre-payment penalties on floating rate loans (prohibited since 2014)
- Not issuing a copy of the loan agreement to the borrower
- Not disclosing processing fees upfront
- Changing interest rates without notice period
- Not informing borrowers before reporting to credit bureaus (CIBIL/CRIF)
