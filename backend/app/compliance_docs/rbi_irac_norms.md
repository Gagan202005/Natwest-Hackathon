# RBI IRAC Norms — Income Recognition and Asset Classification

Source: RBI Master Circular DBR.No.BP.BC.2/21.04.048/2015-16

## NPA Definition

A Non-Performing Asset (NPA) is a loan or advance where:
- Interest and/or instalment of principal remains overdue for more than 90 days (for term loans)
- The account remains "out of order" for more than 90 days (for cash credit/overdraft)
- The bill remains overdue for more than 90 days (for bills purchased/discounted)

## Asset Classification Categories

### Standard Asset
- Account is regular; no overdue payments
- Days Past Due (DPD): 0 days
- No provision required

### Special Mention Accounts (SMA)

SMA-0: DPD 1–30 days (principal or interest overdue up to 30 days)
SMA-1: DPD 31–60 days (principal or interest overdue 31–60 days)
SMA-2: DPD 61–90 days (principal or interest overdue 61–90 days)

SMA accounts are early warning signals and must be monitored closely. Banks must report SMA-2 accounts to CRILC (Central Repository of Information on Large Credits) for exposures above Rs.5 crore.

### Non-Performing Assets

Substandard: NPA for less than or equal to 12 months (DPD 91–365 days)
Doubtful: NPA for more than 12 months (DPD 366–730 days)
Loss: Identified as loss by bank/auditor/RBI inspection (DPD > 730 days or where no recoverable value remains)

## Provisioning Requirements

| Category | Minimum Provision |
|---|---|
| Standard (general) | 0.25–1% |
| SMA-0, SMA-1, SMA-2 | 0% (but monitor) |
| Substandard (secured) | 15% |
| Substandard (unsecured) | 25% |
| Doubtful (up to 1 year) | 25% (secured), 100% (unsecured) |
| Doubtful (1–3 years) | 40% (secured), 100% (unsecured) |
| Doubtful (> 3 years) | 100% |
| Loss | 100% |

## Key Rules for Compliance

1. The 90-day rule is absolute — any loan with DPD > 90 must be classified as at least Substandard
2. Classification must be done on a daily basis (not quarterly)
3. Upgradation from NPA to Standard requires all overdue interest and principal to be fully cleared
4. Once classified as NPA, the asset cannot be upgraded merely by a rescheduling of the loan
5. Interest income on NPA accounts must NOT be recognised on accrual basis

## Common Compliance Failures

- Marking loans as Standard when DPD is 61–90 (these should be SMA-2)
- Not reporting SMA-2 accounts > Rs.5 Cr to CRILC
- Continuing to accrue interest income on NPA accounts
- Failing to make required provisions before the quarter end
