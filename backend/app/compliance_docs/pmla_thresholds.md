# PMLA — Prevention of Money Laundering Act Thresholds & Obligations

Source: PMLA 2002, PML Rules 2005, RBI Master Direction on KYC (2016, updated 2023)

## Cash Transaction Report (CTR)

Banks must file a CTR with the Financial Intelligence Unit — India (FIU-IND) for:
- **All cash transactions of Rs.10 lakh or above** (aggregated in a day for the same customer)
- Transactions in foreign currency of Rs.10 lakh or equivalent
- Multiple cash transactions below Rs.10 lakh that aggregate to Rs.10 lakh or more in a single day for the same customer/account

Filing deadline: By 15th of the succeeding month

## Suspicious Transaction Report (STR)

Banks must file an STR immediately (within 7 working days of suspicion) for:
- Transactions that have no obvious lawful purpose
- Transactions that appear to be structured to avoid reporting thresholds
- Unusual patterns inconsistent with the customer's known business
- Sudden increase in cash transactions without apparent reason
- Multiple transactions just below the threshold (structuring)

## Structuring (Smurfing)

Breaking up a large transaction into multiple smaller transactions to evade the Rs.10 lakh reporting threshold is called "structuring" and is itself a criminal offence under PMLA.

Red flags for structuring:
- Multiple cash deposits of Rs.9.5–9.99 lakh by the same customer within a short period
- Same beneficiary receiving multiple sub-threshold amounts from different accounts
- Round-number deposits just below the Rs.10 lakh threshold

## Non-Profit Organisation (NPO) Monitoring

Banks must apply enhanced due diligence for transactions involving:
- Charities and non-profit organisations
- Cross-border wire transfers from high-risk jurisdictions

## High-Risk Customers (EDD Required)

- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) — domestic and foreign
- High-net-worth individuals with complex structures
- Customers from FATF high-risk countries
- Customers with multiple accounts and frequent inter-account transfers

## Record-Keeping Requirements

- CTR and STR records: Minimum 10 years
- KYC documents: 10 years after end of customer relationship
- Transaction records: 5 years

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

- Fine up to Rs.10 lakh per violation
- Imprisonment of 3–7 years for officers found culpable
- Attachment and confiscation of proceeds of crime
- RBI can impose additional penalties including suspension of banking licence
