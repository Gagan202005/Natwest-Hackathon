# RBI Priority Sector Lending (PSL) Guidelines

Source: RBI Master Direction FIDD.CO.Plan.BC.5/04.09.01/2020-21

## Overall PSL Target

All domestic scheduled commercial banks and foreign banks with 20 or more branches must achieve:
- **40% of Adjusted Net Bank Credit (ANBC)** or Credit Equivalent of Off-Balance Sheet Exposures (CEOBSE), whichever is higher

## Sub-targets

| Sector | Target |
|---|---|
| Agriculture | 18% of ANBC |
| Small and Marginal Farmers | 10% of ANBC (within Agriculture) |
| Micro Enterprises | 7.5% of ANBC |
| Weaker Sections | 12% of ANBC |

## Priority Sector Categories

### Agriculture
- Farm credit: crop loans, agricultural term loans
- Allied activities: dairy, fishery, animal husbandry, poultry
- Agri infrastructure: warehousing, cold chain, soil conservation
- Ancillary activities: food processing, agri-related loans

Loan limits for individual farmers: Up to Rs.2 lakh (indirect) and Rs.10 lakh (for direct agri loans to be classified)

### Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (MSME)
- Micro: Investment up to Rs.1 crore in plant/machinery; turnover up to Rs.5 crore
- Small: Investment up to Rs.10 crore; turnover up to Rs.50 crore
- Medium: Investment up to Rs.50 crore; turnover up to Rs.250 crore

Bank loans to MSME qualify for PSL if:
- Manufacturing/services sector loan to MSME entities
- Loans for food and agro-processing to MSME entities

### Education
- Loans to individuals for educational purposes including vocational courses
- Up to Rs.20 lakh for studies in India
- Up to Rs.30 lakh for studies abroad

### Housing
- Loans up to Rs.35 lakh in metropolitan centres (population >= 10 lakh)
- Loans up to Rs.25 lakh in other centres
- Repair/renovation loans up to Rs.10 lakh (metro) and Rs.6 lakh (others)
- Loans to government agencies for construction of affordable housing

### Weaker Sections
Includes:
- SC/ST borrowers
- Minority communities (as per GoI definition)
- Persons with disabilities
- Women (individual) borrowers
- Overdrafts up to Rs.10,000 under PMJDY
- Distressed farmers indebted to non-institutional lenders
- Self Help Groups (SHGs)
- Persons from DRI scheme
- Minority communities (as per GoI definition)

## Shortfall Consequences

Banks failing to meet PSL targets must deposit shortfall amount in RIDF (Rural Infrastructure Development Fund) or other funds with NABARD/NHB/SIDBI at below-market interest rates.

The penalty is the difference between the rate earned on the shortfall deposits and the market rate.

## Reporting Requirements

- Quarterly reporting to RBI via OSMOS (Online Statistical Monitoring System)
- Annual report by April 30 for the preceding financial year
- Priority Sector certificates maintained for audit purposes
